## Overview

Downtown Vice City is the financial and commercial core of Vice City in GTA 6. The district is defined by towering skyscrapers, commercial buildings, and iconic landmarks. It represents the economic power of Vice City and serves as the most developed and modernized area in the game.

## Key Landmarks

### Twin Towers
The Twin Towers are inspired by Miami's 500 Brickell complex. They feature a distinctive connected rooftop and a large central opening, making them one of the most visually striking structures in Vice City. They appear prominently in official GTA 6 trailers.

### Other Notable Buildings
- **Megamundo Building** - A prominent commercial landmark
- **NINE1NINE** - A high-rise tower
- **Sahara Arena** - A large arena facility
- **Shorefront Building** - A waterfront structure
- **The Sumerian** - An architectural landmark
- **Perez Art Museum-inspired building** - An art museum-style structure
- **Ferris Wheel** - An unnamed observation wheel
- **Amphitheater** - An unnamed outdoor performance venue

## Architecture

The architectural style blends modern Miami aesthetics with combinations of glass, concrete, and metal. The Twin Towers represent the architectural pinnacle of the district, directly referencing Miami's 500 Brickell complex.

## Neighborhood Connections

- Adjacent to Stockyard
- Close to Vice City Port
- Accessible via Ocean Drive

## Trailer Appearances

Downtown Vice City appears in multiple official GTA 6 trailers, showcasing its towering skyline and busy urban streets. The Twin Towers are particularly prominent due to their unique connected rooftop and central opening design.
