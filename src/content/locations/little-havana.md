## Overview

Little Havana, also known as Little Cuba, is Vice City's Cuban cultural center located in the western part of the city. The neighborhood is one of the most culturally distinct areas in Vice City, filled with Spanish-language signage, traditional Cuban architecture, and vibrant cultural activities.

## Culture and Atmosphere

The streets are alive with Cuban and Caribbean atmosphere. Colorful murals cover building surfaces, traditional bars and cafes line the streets, and family-operated shops create a tight-knit community feel. The area reflects the deep roots of Cuban exile culture in Miami, dating back to the 1959 Cuban Revolution.

## Key Landmarks

- **Wynwood Walls-style murals** - Large-scale street art installations inspired by Miami's famous Wynwood Walls mural project
- **Car meet gathering spot** - A location shown in official trailers where car enthusiasts congregate
- **Traditional market** - A food and goods market
- **Panaderias** - Traditional Cuban bakeries selling bread and pastries

## Activities

- **Car meets** - Confirmed in official trailers showing automobile gatherings
- **Street racing** - Racing through community streets
- **Cultural exploration** - Experiencing Cuban traditions
- **Food tasting** - Traditional Cuban food including Panaderias bread and coffee
- **Art appreciation** - Viewing street art and murals

## Architecture

The architectural style blends Cuban traditional and modern Miami elements. Buildings feature bright colors (yellow, pink, blue), wooden shutters, and arched porticos. Street art is a defining characteristic, with colorful murals covering many building surfaces.

## Neighborhood Connections

- West of Vice City center
- Close to Vice City Port
- Adjacent to Downtown and Stockyard
- Connected to Vice City's main transportation network
