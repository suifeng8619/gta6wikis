## Overview

Little Haiti, known in-game as La Perle (meaning "the pearl," from Haiti's French nickname "La Perle des Antilles" — Pearl of the Antilles), is Vice City's Haitian immigrant community center. The neighborhood is defined by colorful murals, traditional Haitian architecture, and a rich Caribbean cultural atmosphere.

## Culture and Music

Little Haiti holds special cultural significance in GTA 6. The inclusion of Haitian Konpa music represents a landmark moment — the first time Konpa music has received such prominent exposure in a AAA video game.

### Konpa Music in GTA 6
The song "Child Support" by Zenglen (featuring Reginald Cange, originally released in 2010) was confirmed playing on a car radio in the GTA 6 trailer, bringing renewed attention to the genre in 2025.

## Key Landmarks

- **Haitian flag mural** - A large-scale mural prominently displaying the Haitian flag
- **Konpa music bars** - Venues for traditional Haitian music performances
- **Traditional market** - Haitian food and goods market
- **Community gathering spots** - Cultural activity centers
- **Food stores** - Shops displaying the Haitian flag

## Architecture

The district blends Haitian traditional and Miami modern styles. Buildings feature vivid colors — particularly blue, red, and yellow associated with the Haitian flag — wooden structures, and simple decoration. Many surfaces are covered with colorful murals and art.

## Activities

- **Cultural exploration** - Experiencing Haitian traditions
- **Food tasting** - Traditional Haitian cuisine
- **Music appreciation** - Konpa music performances (confirmed in trailers)
- **Art viewing** - Murals and street art
- **Shopping** - Traditional goods and food
- **Community interaction** - Cultural NPC interactions

## Neighborhood Connections

- Central Vice City area
- Close to Downtown Vice City
- Adjacent to Little Havana
- Connected to Vice City's main street network

## Trailer Appearances

Little Haiti's presence in official GTA 6 trailers is particularly notable:
- **Haitian flag murals** as prominent visual markers
- **Konpa music** ("Child Support" by Zenglen) playing on a car radio
- **Community murals** decorating walls throughout the district
- **Food stores** with Haitian flag displays
