## Grand Theft Auto: Vice City

Grand Theft Auto: Vice City is the second 3D installment in the GTA series, released in October 2002 for PlayStation 2. Developed by Rockstar North and published by Rockstar Games, the game is set in a fictional version of Miami during the 1986 cocaine era. Vice City is directly relevant to GTA 6, as the new game returns to an expanded Vice City in the modern era.

### Story

Tommy Vercetti (voiced by Ray Liotta), a Forelli Crime Family lieutenant freshly released after 15 years in prison, is sent to Vice City to oversee a drug deal. Ambushed and robbed of $250,000 worth of cocaine, Tommy is stranded without support and must recover the stolen merchandise while building a criminal empire.

The game features 59 main missions (20 required story missions, 22 optional missions, 17 asset missions). Purchasing and managing 7 profitable properties unlocks the final missions.

### Vice City

Set during the peak cocaine epidemic and Miami Vice television era, the city features districts including Downtown, South Beach, Little Havana, Ocean Beach, Vice Point, and The Docks. The game draws heavily from Brian De Palma's 1983 film Scarface, with Tommy Vercetti's journey mirroring Tony Montana's rise to power.

### Music and Radio

Vice City features 9 radio stations with 103 songs spanning 1950s to 1980s music, including Flash FM, V-Rock, Wave 103, Emotion 98.3, Fever 105, Wildstyle Pirate Radio, Radio Espantoso, K-Chat, and VCPR.

### Critical Reception

The game received a Metacritic score of 94/100 on PlayStation 2. It was praised for exceptional voice acting (particularly Ray Liotta's performance), compelling narrative, excellent soundtrack, and sophisticated humor.

### Connection to GTA 6

GTA 6 is set in Vice City during the modern era. The original setting provides historical context, and Tommy Vercetti's legacy affects the modern narrative. V-Rock has been confirmed to return in GTA 6.
