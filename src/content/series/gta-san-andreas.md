## Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas

Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas is the third main 3D entry in the GTA series, released in October 2004 for PlayStation 2. Developed by Rockstar North, the game expanded the scope of open-world games by incorporating RPG-style character development mechanics. Set in a fictional representation of California and Nevada in 1992, the game features street gang culture, state-spanning exploration, and unprecedented character customization.

### Story

Carl "CJ" Johnson returns to Los Santos after his mother's murder, forced back into gang life despite wanting to escape. The story chronicles CJ's struggle to restore the Grove Street Families' power while navigating family conflict, gang rivalry, and systemic corruption. The narrative spans three cities with 101 main story missions.

### Three Cities

- **Los Santos** - Fictional Los Angeles with gang territories (Grove Street, Ballas, Vagos)
- **San Fierro** - Fictional San Francisco with Chinese triad operations
- **Las Venturas** - Fictional Las Vegas with casinos and organized crime

The map is 5x larger than GTA III, featuring urban, suburban, rural, desert, and mountain environments.

### RPG Elements

San Andreas pioneered persistent character attributes:
- **Muscle/Fat/Stamina** - Physical attributes affected by exercise and diet
- **Weapon Skills** - Accuracy improves with use
- **Driving/Pilot Skills** - Vehicle handling improves with practice
- **Clothing and Appearance** - Extensive wardrobe, tattoos, and hairstyle customization
- **Food System** - Diet directly impacts character stats and appearance

### Critical Reception

Metacritic score of 95/100 on PlayStation 2, 90/100 on Windows. Praised for revolutionary scope, character development, RPG integration, and excellent soundtrack.

### Sales

Over 20 million copies sold across all platforms. The game also sparked the Hot Coffee controversy, when hidden sexual content was found by modders, leading to a rating re-evaluation.

### Legacy

San Andreas represents the peak of early 2000s open-world design, pioneering the integration of RPG mechanics into action games. Its approach to character development influenced numerous open-world games that followed.
