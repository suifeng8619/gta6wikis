## Grand Theft Auto III

Grand Theft Auto III is the first fully 3D game in the GTA series, released in October 2001 for PlayStation 2. Developed by Rockstar North and published by Rockstar Games, the game was later released for Windows and Xbox, becoming one of the most influential games of its generation.

### Story

The game follows Claude Speed, a silent protagonist who escapes prison and is betrayed by his girlfriend Catalina during a bank robbery. Claude must build his reputation in the crime-ridden Liberty City, working through multiple crime families including the Italian Mafia, Yakuza, and Colombian cartels.

The game features 67 missions (50 main story + 17 optional Pay Phone Missions), with 10 missable missions that become permanently inaccessible depending on progression order.

### Liberty City

The city is divided into three distinct islands:

- **Portland Island** - Industrial zone and starting area with Mafia compounds
- **Staunton Island** - Urbanized area with Yakuza territory and corporate offices
- **Shoreside Vale** - Suburban areas with Colombian cartel strongholds

### Critical Reception

GTA III received a Metacritic score of 97/100 on PlayStation 2 (the highest-rated PS2 game) and 93/100 on Windows. It is considered a landmark game in establishing the 3D open-world template and among the most significant sixth-generation console games.

### Sales

Over 14.5 million copies sold across all platforms. It was the best-selling game of 2001 and among the best-selling PlayStation 2 games of all time, with over 11.6 million copies on PS2 alone.

### Legacy

GTA III established the 3D open-world formula, demonstrated commercial viability of mature-rated games, pioneered licensed music in game soundtracks, and created the template for sandbox-style gameplay. The Definitive Edition was released as part of GTA: The Trilogy in November 2021.
