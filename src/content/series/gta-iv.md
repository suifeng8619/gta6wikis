## Grand Theft Auto IV

Grand Theft Auto IV is the sixth main entry in the GTA series, released in April 2008 for PlayStation 3 and Xbox 360. It represented a significant technical leap with the introduction of the Euphoria physics engine and explored themes of the American Dream, immigrant experience, and moral corruption.

### Story

Niko Bellic, an Eastern European war veteran from Yugoslavia, emigrates to Liberty City seeking the American Dream, inspired by tales of wealth from his cousin Roman. Upon arrival, Niko discovers Roman's stories were exaggerated -- his cousin lives in debt in a decrepit apartment. Desperate for income, Niko's military background and talent for violence make him invaluable in Liberty City's underworld.

The game features approximately 80 main missions with two possible endings based on player choices. Two substantial DLC expansions -- The Lost and Damned and The Ballad of Gay Tony -- were released as Episodes from Liberty City.

### Liberty City

Redesigned and based on contemporary New York (2008), the city features five boroughs:

- **Broker** - Lower-class neighborhood (Brooklyn equivalent)
- **Algonquin** - Manhattan equivalent with financial district
- **Dukes** - Queens equivalent with suburban neighborhoods
- **Bohan** - Bronx equivalent with industrial areas
- **Alderney** - New Jersey equivalent with Mafia stronghold

### Technical Innovation

The Euphoria physics engine provided dynamic real-time character animation, realistic ragdoll physics, and authentic behavioral responses. This technology influenced subsequent Rockstar games including Red Dead Redemption.

### Critical Reception

Metacritic score of 98/100 on both PlayStation 3 and Xbox 360 -- tied for the highest-rated PS3 game ever. Praised for mature narrative, advanced physics technology, and outstanding voice acting by Michael Hollick as Niko Bellic.

### Sales

Over 25 million copies sold across platforms, generating billions in revenue. The DLC expansions received strong critical reception (Metacritic 87-89/100).
